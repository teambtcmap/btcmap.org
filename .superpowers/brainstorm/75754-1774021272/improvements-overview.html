Map UI Improvement Opportunities

Issues spotted in the current screenshot — click the areas you'd like to focus on

1

Mixed-Language Text

"Boost verloopt in 15 days" — Dutch label, English duration. The opening hours also show English day abbreviations (Mo-Th, Fr-Sa, Su) instead of Dutch (Ma-Do, Vr-Za, Zo). These break the immersion for non-English users.

2

Category Filter Pills Wrap to 3 Rows

8 category pills (Alle, Restaurants, Winkelen, Boodschappen, Koffie, Geldautomaten, Hotels, Schoonheidssalons) wrap to 3 lines, consuming ~80px of vertical space. A horizontally scrollable single row or compact icon-only pills would reclaim space for the merchant list.

3

Drawer Content Length & Scrolling

The merchant detail drawer is very tall — action buttons + payment methods + verification + boost + 4 comments pushes content well below the fold. The merchant name scrolls off-screen. A sticky header and collapsible comments section would help.

4

Action Buttons Take Too Much Space

The 2×2 grid of large action buttons (Navigeren, Bewerk, Deel, Opmerkingen) dominates the top of the drawer. These could be an inline icon row or a compact toolbar to free up space for the actual merchant info.

Additional Observations

5

Location Permission Banner

"Maak nauwkeurige afstanden mogelijk" takes up a full row in the sidebar. Once dismissed, it doesn't return, but for first-time visitors it pushes the merchant list down. Could be integrated into the search bar or shown as a subtle icon.

6

Map Visible Area When Drawer Open

With sidebar (320px) + drawer (400px) both open, ~720px of horizontal space is consumed on the left. On a 1366px laptop screen, more than half the map is hidden. Consider overlaying the drawer on the sidebar or using a slide-over pattern.

7

Comments Section UX

All 4 comments are shown inline, taking ~200px of vertical space. The oldest are 2+ years old. Consider: collapsed by default with "Show 4 comments" expand, or showing only the most recent 1-2 with a "show more" link.

8

No Marker Legend on Map

The map uses different colored icons (teal squares, orange markers, green diamonds) for different merchant types, but there's no legend explaining what each color/shape means. New users may not understand the visual encoding.
